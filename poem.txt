When morning light breaks over quiet hills
And golden sun slips through the swaying leaves,
The world stirs soft — a bird calls, cool air fills
The open sky, and shakes the tallest trees.

I think about the hours we let pass,
The talks we never had, the rush, the noise,
The way you laughed in summer on the grass
Before we knew how brief are simple joys.

But standing here, I find that time is kind —
It does not only take, it also leaves
A sense of what we carry and what binds
Us to this world, like roots beneath the eaves.

  So hold the light that every morning gives,
  And in the small and quiet moments, live.

For we are brief, like candles in the wind,
Our names will fade like footprints in the rain,
Yet what we loved, and gave, and felt within
Will echo on in those we touched with grace.

The child who watched the sparrows from the sill,
The friend who stayed when all the others left,
The stranger's smile that bent against your will —
These are the things that keep us from the bereft.

So do not mourn the years already gone,
Nor dread the ones that wait beyond the bend.
Each day is both a dusk and a new dawn,
Each breath a small beginning, not an end.

  We are the sum of all we chose to see,
  And all we loved is all we'll ever be.
